Problem 1

Given: $ABCD$ is a rectangle. $M$ is the midpoint of $[AB]$, $N$ is the midpoint of $[BC]$, and $O$ is the midpoint of diagonal $[AC]$.

Show that: $OMBN$ is a rectangle.

Step 1: In $\triangle ABC$, $M$ is the midpoint of $AB$ and $O$ is the midpoint of $AC$. By the [Midpoint Theorem], $[MO] \parallel [BC]$ and $MO = \frac{1}{2}BC$.

Step 2: Since $N$ is the midpoint of $BC$, we have $BN = \frac{1}{2}BC$. Thus, $MO = BN$ and $[MO] \parallel [BN]$. This proves $OMBN$ is a parallelogram.

Step 3: Similarly, in $\triangle ABC$, $O$ and $N$ are midpoints of $AC$ and $BC$. Therefore $[ON] \parallel [AB]$ and $ON = \frac{1}{2}AB$.

Step 4: Since $ABCD$ is a rectangle, $\widehat{ABC} = 90^\circ$.

Conclusion: A parallelogram with at least one right angle is a rectangle. Therefore, $OMBN$ is a rectangle.

Problem 2

Given: $ABCD$ is a square. $M$ and $N$ are points on diagonal $[BD]$ such that $BN = DM$.

Show that: $ANCM$ is a rhombus.

Step 1: In a square, diagonals bisect each other at right angles. Let $O$ be the intersection of diagonals $AC$ and $BD$. Thus, $OA = OC$ and $OB = OD$.

Step 2: Since $BN = DM$ and $OB = OD$, it follows that $OB - BN = OD - DM$, which means $ON = OM$.

Step 3: In quadrilateral $ANCM$, the diagonals $AC$ and $MN$ bisect each other ($OA=OC$ and $OM=ON$). This proves $ANCM$ is a parallelogram.

Step 4: Because the diagonals of the square are perpendicular ($AC \perp BD$), the diagonals of $ANCM$ are also perpendicular.

Conclusion: A parallelogram whose diagonals are perpendicular is a **rhombus**. Thus, $ANCM$ is a rhombus.

Problem 3

Given: $ABCD$ is a quadrilateral with perpendicular diagonals $[AC]$ and $[BD]$. $S, T, U, V$ are the midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$.

Show that: $STUV$ is a rectangle.

Step 1: By the [Midpoint Theorem] in $\triangle ABC$, $[ST] \parallel [AC]$ and $ST = \frac{1}{2}AC$. In $\triangle ADC$, $[VU] \parallel [AC]$ and $VU = \frac{1}{2}AC$.

Step 2: Thus $[ST] \parallel [VU]$ and $ST = VU$, making $STUV$ a parallelogram.

Step 3: Similarly, $SV \parallel BD$ because $S$ and $V$ are midpoints in $\triangle ABD$.

Step 4: Since $AC \perp BD$ (given), and $[ST] \parallel [AC]$ and $[SV] \parallel [BD]$, it follows that $ST \perp SV$.

Conclusion: A parallelogram with a right angle is a **rectangle**. Therefore, $STUV$ is a rectangle.

Problem 4

Given: $ABCD$ is a quadrilateral with equal diagonals ($AC = BD$). $M, N, P, Q$ are the midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$.

Show that: $MNPQ$ is a rhombus.

Step 1: By the [Midpoint Theorem] in $\triangle ABC$, $MN = \frac{1}{2}AC$. In $\triangle ADC$, $PQ = \frac{1}{2}AC$. Thus $MN = PQ$.

Step 2: In $\triangle ABD$, $MQ = \frac{1}{2}BD$. In $\triangle BCD$, $NP = \frac{1}{2}BD$. Thus $MQ = NP$.

Step 3: We are given that the diagonals are equal ($AC = BD$).

Step 4: Substituting the equality, we get $MN = \frac{1}{2}AC = \frac{1}{2}BD = MQ$.

Conclusion: Since $MN = NP = PQ = QM$, the parallelogram $MNPQ$ has four equal sides, which defines a **rhombus**.

Problem 5

Given: $ABC$ is a right isosceles triangle with vertex $A$. $I, J, K$ are the respective midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC],$ and $[AC]$.

Show that: $AIJK$ is a square.

Step 1: By the [Midpoint Theorem], $JK \parallel AB$ and $JK = \frac{1}{2}AB$. Since $I$ is the midpoint of $AB$, $AI = \frac{1}{2}AB$. Thus, $[JK] \parallel [AI]$ and $JK = AI$, making $AIJK$ a parallelogram.

Step 2: Similarly, $[IJ] \parallel [AC]$ and $IJ = \frac{1}{2}AC$. Since $K$ is the midpoint of $AC$, $AK = \frac{1}{2}AC$. Thus $[IJ] \parallel [AK]$ and $IJ = AK$.

Step 3: Since $\triangle ABC$ is a right triangle at $A$, $\widehat{IAK} = 90^\circ$. A parallelogram with a right angle is a rectangle.

Step 4: In an isosceles triangle with $AB = AC$, their halves are also equal: $AI = \frac{1}{2}AB = \frac{1}{2}AC = AK$.

Conclusion: A rectangle with two adjacent equal sides ($AI = AK$) is a **square**. Therefore, $AIJK$ is a square.

P1. Forming a rectangle within a rhombus

Given: $ABCD$ is a rhombus. $M, N, P,$ and $Q$ are the midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$ respectively.

Show that: $MNPQ$ is a rectangle.

Instruction: Apply the Midpoint Theorem to show $MNPQ$ is a parallelogram. Recall that the diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular to prove that this parallelogram has a right angle.
P2. Identifying a rhombus in an isosceles trapezoid

Given: $ABCD$ is an isosceles trapezoid with $[AB] \parallel [CD]$ and non-parallel legs $AD = BC$. $M, N, P, Q$ are the midpoints of the sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$.

Show that: $MNPQ$ is a rhombus.

Instruction: First, establish that $MNPQ$ is a parallelogram using the Midpoint Theorem. Then, use the property that an isosceles trapezoid has equal diagonals ($AC=BD$) to prove all four sides of $MNPQ$ are equal in length.
P3. Analyzing a general quadrilateral with equal diagonals

Given: $ABCD$ is a general quadrilateral with equal diagonals ($AC = BD$). $S, T, U, V$ are the midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$.

Show that: $STUV$ is a rhombus.

Instruction: Use the Midpoint Theorem in $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle ADC$ to find the lengths of $ST$ and $VU$. Similarly, analyze $\triangle ABD$ and $\triangle BCD$. The fact that $AC=BD$ will prove all four sides of $STUV$ are equal.
P4. The figure formed by midpoints of a kite

Given: $ABCD$ is a kite with adjacent sides $AB=AD$ and $CB=CD$. $P, Q, R, S$ are the midpoints of sides $[AB], [BC], [CD],$ and $[DA]$.

Show that: $PQRS$ is a rectangle.

Instruction: A kite has perpendicular diagonals. Use the Midpoint Theorem to relate the sides of $PQRS$ to the diagonals of the kite. A parallelogram with perpendicular diagonals is a rectangle.
P5. A right triangle variation

Given: $\triangle LMN$ is a right triangle at $M$. $P, Q, R$ are the midpoints of sides $[LM], [MN],$ and $[NL]$.

Show that: $MPQR$ is a rectangle.

Instruction: Use the Midpoint Theorem to show that $PQ \parallel LM$ and $QR \parallel MN$. Since the original angle at $M$ is $90^\circ$, this directly proves $MPQR$ is a parallelogram with a right angle, thus a rectangle.
