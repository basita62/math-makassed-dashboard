Logarithmic and exponential functions ln(x) and exp(x)


  • $\cfrac{d}{dx}(\ln|x|) = \cfrac{1}{x}$ ; $\cfrac{d}{dx}\ln|u| = \cfrac{u'}{u}$
  • $\ln(a \times b) = \ln a + \ln b$, where $a > 0$ and $b > 0$
  • $\ln\left(\frac{a}{b}\right) = \ln a - \ln b$, where $a > 0$ and $b > 0$
  • $\ln\left(\frac{1}{a}\right) = - \ln a$, where $a > 0$
  • $\ln a^n = n \ln a$, where $a > 0$ and $n$ is real
  • $\ln 1 = 0$ ; $\ln e = 1$ ; $\ln e^2 = 2$
  • $\ln e^n = n$ ; $\ln \frac{1}{e} = -1$
  • $a = \ln e^a = e^{\ln a}$
  • $\ln x = a \iff x = e^a$
  • If $e^x = a$ then $x = \ln a$ $\thinspace$ where $a > 0$
  • $\ln a = \ln b \iff a = b$, where $a > 0$ and $b > 0$
  • $\ln a < \ln b \iff a < b$, where $a > 0$ and $b > 0$
  • $\ln a > \ln b \iff a > b$, where $a > 0$ and $b > 0$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to 0^+} \ln x = -\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \ln x = +\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to 0^+} x \ln x = 0^-$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{\ln x}{x} = 0$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{x}{\ln x} = +\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to 0^+} x^n \ln x = 0^-$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{\ln x}{x^n} = 0^+$ where $n > 0$.
  • The function: $x \mapsto \ln x$ has domain = ]0; +$\infty$[ and range = ]$\infty$; +$\infty$[.
  • The function: $x \mapsto e^x$ has domain = ]$\infty$; +$\infty$[ and range = ]0; +$\infty$[.
  • These two functions are inverses of each other. Their graphs are symmetrical with respect to the first bisector ($y = x$).
  • $y = \ln x \iff x = e^y$
  • $e^a = e^b \iff a = b$
  • $e^a < e^b \iff a < b$
  • $e^a > e^b \iff a > b$
  • $e^a \cdot e^b = e^{a+b}$
  • $\frac{e^a}{e^b} = e^{a-b}$
  • $(e^a)^b = e^{ab}$
  • $e^{-a} = \frac{1}{e^a}$
  • $\frac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x \text{ and } \frac{d}{dx}(e^u) = e^u \cdot u'$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty} e^x = 0^+$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} e^x = +\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{e^x}{x} = +\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{x}{e^x} = 0$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to 0} \frac{e^x-1}{x} = 1$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} e^{-x} = 0$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to +\infty} \frac{e^x}{x^n} = +\infty$
  • $\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty} x^n e^x = 0$ , ($n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$)

Derivatives and Antiderivatives rules

  • L'Hopital's Rule for limits of indeterminate forms $\cfrac{0}{0}$ or $\cfrac{\infty}{\infty}:$
  • If $\lim\limits_{x\to a} \cfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ exists, then $\lim\limits_{x\to a} \cfrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$ exists, then $\lim\limits_{x\to a} \cfrac{f(x)}{g(g)} = \lim\limits_{x\to a} \cfrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$
  • $F(x)$ is an anti-derivative $G(x) \iff F'(x) = G(x)$
  • $F(x) = \displaystyle\int G(x)dx \iff F'(x) = G(x)$.
  • $\cfrac{d}{dx} (\displaystyle\int f(x)dx) = f(x)$ and $\displaystyle\int f'(x)dx = f(x) + C$
  • $\displaystyle\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n + 1} + C$ $\text{ }$ ($n \neq -1$)
  • $\displaystyle\int(u \pm v) = \displaystyle\int u \pm \displaystyle\int v$
  • $\displaystyle\int ku = k \displaystyle\int u$ $\text{ ; }$ $k \in \mathbb{R}$
  • $\displaystyle\int u'v = uv - \displaystyle\int v'u$ $\text{ }$ (integration by parts)
  • $\displaystyle\int u'f(u)dx = \displaystyle\int f(u)du$
  • $f_x' = f_u' \times u_x'$ (chain rule: $\cfrac{df}{dx} = \cfrac{df}{du} \times \cfrac{du}{dx}$)
  • $(u \pm v)' = u' \pm v'$
  • $(uv)' = u'v + v'u$
  • $\left(\cfrac{u}{v}\right)' = \cfrac{u'v - v'u}{v^2}$
  • $\left(\cfrac{1}{u}\right)' = - \cfrac{u'}{u}$

Definite Integrals and Applications

  • If $F$ is an anti-derivative of $f$ over $I$, then $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)$.
  • $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)dx$ = algebraic area of the domain limited by the representative curve of $f$, the x-axis and the two straight lines of equations $x = a$ and $x = b$.
  • Mean value of a function (average value) over an interval $[a; b]$ is $\bar{f} = \frac{1}{b-a}\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)dx$.
  • $\displaystyle\int_a^b (f(x) - g(x))dx$ = area of the domain limited by the representative curves of $f$ and $g$ where $f(x) > g(x)$ over $[a, b]$ and the two straight lines of equations $x = a$ and $x = b$.
  • $\displaystyle\int_b^a f(x)dx = - \displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)dx$
  • $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)dx = \displaystyle\int_a^c f(x)dx + \displaystyle\int_c^b f(x)dx$
  • $\displaystyle\int_a^b k dx = k(b - a)$ and $\displaystyle\int_a^b dx = b - a$ and $\displaystyle\int_a^a dx = 0$.
  • If $f(x)$ is an odd function, then $\displaystyle\int_{-a}^a f(x)dx = 0$, $a \in \mathbb{R}$.
  • If $f(x)$ is an even function, then $\displaystyle\int_{-a}^a f(x)dx = 2 \times \displaystyle\int_0^a f(x)dx$, $a \in \mathbb{R}$.
  • If $f(x) \leq g(x)$, then $\displaystyle\int f(x)dx \leq \displaystyle\int g(x)dx$
  • If $H(x) = \displaystyle\int_a^{u(x)} f(t)dt$, then $H'(x) = f(u(x)) \times u'(x)$, $a \in \mathbb{R}$

Special economics

  • Given the cost function : $C_T(x)$, then marginal cost function : $C_m(x) = C_T'(x)$
  • Given marginal cost function : $C_m(x)$, then total cost function : $C_T(x) = \displaystyle\int C_m(x)dx + K$.

Lines and planes

1) Scalar product (or dot product)

  • Geometric form: $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \|\vec{u}\| \cdot \|\vec{v}\| \cdot \cos(\vec{u}, \vec{v})$.
  • Angle between two vectors : $\cos(\vec{u}, \vec{v}) = \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{\|\vec{u}\| \cdot \|\vec{v}\|}$
  • Basic properties :
    • $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are orthogonal iff $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0$
    • $\vec{u}^2 = \|\vec{u}\|^2$
    • $(\vec{u} + \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{w} = \vec{u} \cdot \vec{w} + \vec{v} \cdot \vec{w}$
    • $k(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}) = (k\vec{u}) \cdot \vec{v} = \vec{u} \cdot (k\vec{v})$ ; $k \in \mathbb{R}$
    • $\|\vec{u} + \vec{v}\| \leq \|\vec{u}\| + \|\vec{v}\|$
  • Analytic form :
    In an orthonormal system ($O; \vec{i}, \vec{j}, \vec{k}$) where $\vec{u}(x, y, z)$ and $\vec{v}(x', y', z')$ : $$\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = xx' + yy' + zz'$$
  • Magnitude of a vector : $\|\vec{u}\| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$
  • Angle between two vectors : $$\cos(\vec{u}, \vec{v}) = \frac{xx' + yy' + zz'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \cdot \sqrt{x'^2 + y'^2 + z'^2}}$$

2) Vector product (or cross product)

  • Geometric form :
    If $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are collinear, then, $\vec{u} \times \vec{v} = \vec{0}$.
    If $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are not collinear, then, $\vec{u} \times \vec{v} = \|\vec{u}\| \cdot \|\vec{v}\| \cdot \sin(\vec{u}, \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{n}$
    where $\vec{n}$ is a unit vector perpendicular to the plane formed by $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ and such that ($\vec{u}, \vec{v}, \vec{n}$) form a direct base (or right handed base).
  • Basic properties :
    • $\|\vec{u} \times \vec{v}\| = \|\vec{u}\| \cdot \|\vec{v}\| \cdot |\sin(\vec{u}, \vec{v})|$
    • $\vec{v} \times \vec{u} = - \vec{u} \times \vec{v}$
    • $(\vec{u} + \vec{v}) \times \vec{w} = \vec{u} \times \vec{w} + \vec{v} \times \vec{w}$
    • $k(\vec{u} \times \vec{v}) = (k\vec{u}) \times \vec{v} = \vec{u} \times (k\vec{v})$ ; $k \in \mathbb{R}$
  • Analytic form :
    In a direct orthonormal system ($O; \vec{i}, \vec{j}, \vec{k}$) where $\vec{u}(x, y, z)$ and $\vec{v}(x', y', z')$ : $$\vec{u} \times \vec{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ x & y & z \\ x' & y' & z' \end{vmatrix}$$
  • Area of parallelogram with sides $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ : Area = $\|\vec{u} \times \vec{v}\|$
  • Area of parallelogram ABCD : Area = $\|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD}\|$
  • Area of triangle ABC : Area = $\frac{1}{2}\|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}\|$
    This is an alternative method to the classical rule : $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$$
  • Volume of parallelepiped with sides $\vec{u}, \vec{v}$ and $\vec{w}$ : $V = (\vec{u} \times \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{w}$
  • Volume of parallelepiped ABCDEFGH : $V = (\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AE}$
  • Volume of tetrahedron ABCD : $V = \frac{1}{6}(\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AD}$
    This is an alternative method to the classical rule : $$\text{Volume} = \frac{1}{3} \times \text{area of base} \times \text{height of tetrahedron}$$
  • Proving 3 points $A, B$ and $C$ collinear : $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} = \vec{0}$
  • Proving 3 vectors $\vec{u}, \vec{v}$ and $\vec{w}$ coplanar : $(\vec{u} \times \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{w} = 0$
  • Proving 4 points $A, B, C$ and $D$ coplanar : $\det(\overrightarrow{AB}, \overrightarrow{AC}, \overrightarrow{AD}) = 0$
  • Proving 4 points $A, B, C$ and $D$ coplanar : $(\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AD} = 0$
  • A normal vector to the plane ($ABC$) is : $\vec{n} = \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}$
  • Equation of plane with normal vector $\vec{n}(u, v, w)$ and passing through point $A$ :
    $\overrightarrow{AM} \cdot \vec{n} = 0$ where $M(x, y, z)$ is a point on the plane.
    That is equivalent to : $\thinspace$ $ux + vy + wz + r = 0$
  • Parametric equations of a straight line with directing vector $\vec{S}(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ and passing through point $A$ : \[ \begin{cases} x = \alpha t + x_A \\ y = \beta t + y_A \\ z = \gamma t + z_A \end{cases} ; t \in \mathbb{R} \]
  • Distance between point $A$ and line ($D$) of direction vector $\vec{S}$ with $B \in (D)$ : $$d(A, (D)) = \cfrac{\|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \vec{S}\|}{\|\vec{S}\|}$$
  • Distance between point $P$ and plane ($ABC$) : $$d(P, (ABC)) = \cfrac{\left|(\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AP}\right|}{\left\|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}\right\|}$$ Note: this is the length of the height of the tetrahedron PABC isued from P.
    That is equivalent to : $\thinspace$ $d(P, (ABC)) = \cfrac{\left|\vec{n} \cdot \overrightarrow{AP}\right|}{\left\|\vec{n}\right\|}$ $\thinspace$ where: $\vec{n}$ is a normal vector to ($ABC$)
    That is equivalent to : $\thinspace$ $d(P, (ABC)) = \cfrac{\left|ux_p + vy_p + wz_p + r\right|}{\sqrt{u^2 + v^2 + w^2}}$
