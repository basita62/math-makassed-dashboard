1. Arithmetic Sequences

Focus: Common Difference $d$, General Term ($a_n$), and Sum $S_n$.

10 Solved Examples

1. $\text{Find the tenth term of an arithmetic sequence of first term 5 and} \\ \quad \text{common difference 3.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_{10} = a_{1} + (10-1)d = 5+(9)(3) = 32$
2. $\text{Find the sum of the first 15 terms term of an arithmetic sequence} \\ \quad \text{of first term 2 and common difference 2.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\S_{15} = \cfrac{15}{2}(2a_1 + (15-1)d) = \cfrac{15}{2}(4 + 14\times2) = 240$
3. $\text{Find the common difference of an arithmetic sequence of first term} \\ \quad \text{17 and tenth term 71.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_{10} = a_{1} + (10-1)d \\ 71 = 17 + 9d \\ d = 6$
4. $\text{Find the number of terms of the arithmetic sequence 13,17,...,137.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_n = a_{1} + (n-1)d \\ 137=13+(n-1)(4) \\ n = 32$
5. $\text{Find the first term of an arithmetic sequence of fifth term 36 and} \\ \quad \text{the sum of the first five terms is 220.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ S_{5} = \cfrac{5}{2}(a_1 + a_5) \\ 220 = \cfrac{5}{2}(a_1+36)\\ a_ 1= 52$
6. $\text{Find the sum of the first 100 positive integers.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\S_{100} = \cfrac{100}{2}(a_1 + a_{100}) = \cfrac{100}{2}(1+100)= 5050$
7. $\text{Find the common difference of an arithmetic sequence of 10th term} \\ \quad \text{20 and 30th term 60.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_{30} = a_{10} + (30-10)d \\ d = \cfrac{60-20}{20} = 2$
8. $\text{Find the 90th term of an arithmetic sequence of 17th term 36} \\ \quad \text{and common difference 5.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_{90} = a_{17} + (90-17)(5) = 36 + 365 = 401$
9. $\text{Find the general term of an arithmetic sequence 10,7,4,... } \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_n = a_{1} + (n-1)d = 10+(n-1)(-3) = -3n+13$
10. $\text{Find the value of } x \text{ if } x,2x+1,4x-2 \text{ form an arithmetic sequence}. \\ \text{Solution:} \\2x+1=\cfrac{x+4x-2}{2} \\4x+2=5x-2 \\x=4$

2. Geometric Sequences

Focus: Common Ratio $r$, General Term $ar^{n-1}$, and Sum $S_n$.

10 Solved Examples

1. $\text{Find the 12th term of a geometric sequence of first term 80 and } \\ \quad \text{common ratio 0.5 .} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_{10} = a_{1}r^{12-1} = 80(0.5)^{11} \approx 0.039$
2. $\text{Find the sum of the first 10 terms of the geometric sequence} \\ \quad 10,30,90,... \\ \text{Solution:} \\S_{10} = a_1 \times \cfrac{1-r^{10}}{1-r} = 10\times \cfrac{1-3^{10}}{1-3} = 295240$
3. $\text{Find the common ratio of a geometric sequence of second term} \\ \quad \text{9.6 and fourth term 6.144 .} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_4 = a_2 \times r^{4-2} \\ r^2 = \cfrac{6.144}{9.6} = 0.64 \\r= \pm 0.8$
4. $\text{Find the sum to infinity of the geometric sequence 16, 8, 4...} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ S_\infty = \cfrac{a_1}{1-r} = \cfrac{16}{1-\frac{1}{2}} = 32$
5. $\text{Find the number of terms of a geometric sequence of first term 6,} \\ \quad \text{common ratio 2 and sum of terms 1572858.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\S_n = a_1 \times \cfrac{1-r^n}{1-r} \\ 1572858 = 6\times \cfrac{1-2^n}{1-2} \\ 262143 = 2^n -1 \\ 2^n = 262144 \\ n = 18$
6. $\text{Find the general term of the geometric sequence 5, 10, 20...} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_n = a_{1}r^{n-1} = 5(2)^{n-1}$
7. $\text{Find the first term of a geometric sequence of fifth term 48 and} \\ \quad \text{common ratio 2.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\a_5 = a_{1}r^{5-1} \\ 48 = a_1 \times 2^4 \\ a_1 = 3$
8. $\text{Find the value of } x \text{ if } x,x+3,4x \text{ form a geometric sequence}. \\ \text{Solution:} \\(x+3)^2 = (x)(4x) \\ (x+3)^2=4x^2 \\x+3=2x \text{ or } x+3=-2x \\x=3 \text{ or } x=-1$
9. $\text{Find the sum of the first 25 terms of the geometric sequence} \\ \quad 1, 2, 4, 8... \\ \text{Solution:} \\S_{25} = a_1 \times \cfrac{1-r^{25}}{1-r} = 1\times \cfrac{1-2^{25}}{1-2} = 33554431$
10. $\text{Find the common ratio of a geometric sequence of first term} \\ \quad \text{20480 and sum to infinity 81920.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ S_\infty = \cfrac{a_1}{1-r} \\ 81920 = \cfrac{20480}{1-r} \\ 1-r=\cfrac{20480}{81920} \\ r=0.75$

3. Real-Life Problems (2026 Context)

Applying Arithmetic and Geometric models to finance, physics, and growth.

10 Solved Examples

1. Salary (AS):
$ \text{A job starts at 40,000 dollars with a 2,500 dollars annual raise.} \\ \text{Find salary in year 10.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 40000 + (9)2500 = 62,500 \text{ dollars}.$
2. Savings (GS):
$ \text{You deposit 1,000 dollars at 5\% annual interest.} \\ \text{Find balance after 8 years.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 1000(1.05)^8 = 1,477.46 \text{ dollars}.$
3. Construction (AS):
$ \text{A stadium has 20 seats in row 1, and each row adds 4 seats.} \\ \text{How many in row 30?} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 20 + (29)4 = 136 \text{ seats}.$
4. Biology (GS):
$ \text{Bacteria doubles every hour. If you start with 50,} \\ \text{how many after 12 hours?} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 50(2)^{12} = 204,800.$
5. Logistics (AS):
$ \text{A truck drives 500km on day 1, increasing by 50km daily.} \\ \text{What is the total distance in 10 days?} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ S_{10} = \cfrac{10}{2}(2(500) + 9(50)) = 7,250\text{ km}.$
6. Tech (GS):
$ \text{A phone's value 2000 dollars depreciates by 20\% yearly.} \\ \text{What is its value after 4 years?} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 2000(0.80)^4 = 819.20 \text{ dollars}.$
7. Fitness (AS):
$ \text{If you run 2km on day 1, add 0.5km daily.} \\ \text{On which day do you run 10km?} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 10 = 2 + (n-1)0.5 \\ n=17.$
8. Finance (GS):
$ \text{A 2026 startup's profit triples every year.} \\ \text{If Year 1 is \$5000, find Year 6. } \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 5000(3)^5 = \$1,215,000.$
9. Physics (AS):
$ \text{An object falls 4.9m after 1 second, 14.7m after 2 seconds,} \\ \text{24.5m after 3 seconds.} \\ \text{Find distance after 10 seconds.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ 4.9 + (9)9.8 = 93.1\text{ m}.$
10. Marketing (GS):
$ \text{A video gets 100 views, then increases by 50\% daily.} \\ \text{Find the total views in 5 days.} \\ \text{Solution:} \\ S_5 = \cfrac{100(1.5^5 - 1)}{1.5 - 1} = 1,318.75 \approx 1,319.$

4. Sequences Defined by General Term

Identify the sequence type and first term ($a_1$) from the explicit formula $a_n$.

10 Solved Examples

1. $a_n = 4n + 3$: Find $a_1$ and type.
$\rightarrow a_1 = 4(1)+3 = 7$. (AS, $d=4$)
2. $a_n = 3(2^n)$: Find $a_1$ and type.
$\rightarrow a_1 = 3(2^1) = 6$. (GS, $r=2$)
3. $a_n = 10 - 2n$: Find $a_1$ and $a_{10}$.
$\rightarrow a_1 = 8, a_{10} = -10$. (AS, $d=-2$)
4. $a_n = 5^{n-1}$: Find $a_1$ and $r$.
$\rightarrow a_1 = 1, r=5$. (GS)
5. $a_n = \frac{n+1}{2}$: Find $a_1, a_2$.
$\rightarrow a_1 = 1, a_2 = 1.5$. (AS, $d=0.5$)
6. $a_n = (-1)^n \cdot 4$: Find first 3 terms.
$\rightarrow -4, 4, -4$. (GS, $r=-1$)
7. $a_n = 7n$: Find sum of first 5 terms.
$\rightarrow 7, 14, 21, 28, 35 \rightarrow \text{Sum} = 105$. (AS)
8. $a_n = \frac{1}{2^n}$: Is this GS?
$\rightarrow a_1=0.5, a_2=0.25$. Yes, $r=0.5$.
9. $a_n = 2n^2$: Is this AS or GS?
$\rightarrow a_1=2, a_2=8, a_3=18$. Neither.
10. $a_n = 100(0.5)^{n-1}$: Find $a_5$.
$\rightarrow 100(0.5)^4 = 6.25$. (GS)

5. Recurrence Relations: $a_{n+1} = ra_n + d$

Focus: Calculating terms and relating them to Geometric/Arithmetic foundations.

10 Solved Examples

1. $a_{n+1}=3a_n+5, a_0=4$: Find $a_1, a_2$.
$\rightarrow a_1=3(4)+5=17$. $a_2=3(17)+5=56$.
Relate: Shifts a GS ($r=3$) by a fixed point $L=-2.5$.
2. $a_{n+1}=2a_n-1, a_1=5$: Find $a_2, a_3$.
$\rightarrow a_2=2(5)-1=9$. $a_3=2(9)-1=17$.
3. $a_{n+1}=a_n+7, a_0=10$: Find type.
$\rightarrow$ Since $r=1$, this is a pure Arithmetic Sequence ($d=7$).
4. $a_{n+1}=4a_n, a_1=2$: Find type.
$\rightarrow$ Since $d=0$, this is a pure Geometric Sequence ($r=4$).
5. $a_{n+1}=0.5a_n+10, a_0=20$: Find $a_1, a_2$.
$\rightarrow a_1=0.5(20)+10=20$. $a_2=20$. (Constant sequence).
6. $a_{n+1}=-a_n+4, a_0=1$: Find $a_1, a_2, a_3$.
$\rightarrow 3, 1, 3$. (Oscillating sequence).
7. $a_{n+1}=1.1a_n+100, a_0=1000$: Find $a_1$.
$\rightarrow 1.1(1000)+100=1200$. (Models growth + deposit).
8. $a_{n+1}=3a_n-6, a_0=3$: Find $a_1, a_2$.
$\rightarrow a_1=3(3)-6=3$. $a_2=3$. (Fixed point at 3).
9. $a_{n+1}=\frac{1}{3}a_n+2, a_0=6$: Find $a_1, a_2$.
$\rightarrow a_1=2+2=4$. $a_2=\frac{4}{3}+2=\frac{10}{3}$.
10. $a_{n+1}=5a_n-20, a_1=5$: Find $a_2, a_3$.
$\rightarrow a_2=5(5)-20=5$. $a_3=5$.
