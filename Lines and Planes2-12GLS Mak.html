Problem 01

Shortest Distance Between Skew Lines

$\text{Given:} \\$ $(L_1): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= t \\ y &= 0.5t + 2 \\ z &= 2 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R} \quad \text{ ; } \quad (L_2): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= 1 \\ y &= 4m \\ z &= m - 2 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } m\in\mathbb{R}$
$\text{Find the shortest distance between}$ $(L_1)$ and $(L_2)$.

Step 1

Find the equation of the plane $(P)$ containing $(L_2)$ and parallel to $(L_1)$.
$\vec{n}=\vec{S_1}\times\vec{S_2}=(0.5,-1,4)$ and passes through point C(1,0,-2).
So \((P)\): $\thinspace 0.5x-y+4z+7.5=0$

Step 2

Project any point $A$ from $(L_1)$ onto $(P)$ to find point $B$.
$A(0,2,2)$ belongs to $(L_1)$. The line through $A$ and perpedicular to $(P)$ is:
$(D): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= 0.5k \\ y &= -k + 2 \\ z &= 4k + 2 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } k\in\mathbb{R}$
Substituting in $(P)$: $\thinspace 0.5(0.5k)-(-k+2)+4(4k+2)+7.5=0$
then $\thinspace k=-\cfrac{18}{23}$
Substitute back in the equations of $(D)$ to get $B(-\frac{9}{23}, \frac{64}{23}, -\frac{26}{23})$

Step 3

Shortest distance is: $\thinspace d = \|\overrightarrow{AB}\| = \sqrt{\frac{5589}{529}} \approx 3.25$.

Problem 02

Intersection of Two Planes

Find the parametric equations of the straight line formed by the intersection of the following two planes: \[ P_1: x + y + z = 1 \] \[ P_2: x - 2y + 3z = 1 \]

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder]

Step 1

Find the Direction Vector: The direction of the line is the cross product of the normals \(\vec{n_1} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle\) and \(\vec{n_2} = \langle 1, -2, 3 \rangle\): \[ \mathbf{v} = \vec{n_1} \times \vec{n_2} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 3 \end{vmatrix} = \langle 5, -2, -3 \rangle \]

Step 2

Find a Common Point: Set \(z = 0\) to solve the system: \[ \begin{cases} x + y = 1 \\ x - 2y = 1 \end{cases} \] Subtracting the equations yields \(3y = 0 \Rightarrow y = 0\). Substituting back gives \(x = 1\). Point: \((1, 0, 0)\).

Step 3

Parametric Equations: Combine the point and direction vector: $(D): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= 1 + 5t \\ y &= -2t \\ z &= -3t \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R}$

Problem 03

Equation of Plane Containing a Line and Perpendicular to Another Plane

Find the equation of the plane that contains the line $(L)$ given by the parametric equations: $$(L): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= 2t + 1 \\ y &= -t - 2 \\ z &= 3t \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R}$$ and is perpendicular to the plane $(P)$: $x + 2y + z = 5$.

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder]

Step 1

Extract Vectors: Direction vector of line $(L)$: $\vec{v} = \langle 2, -1, 3 \rangle$. Normal vector of plane $(P)$: $\vec{n}_1 = \langle 1, 2, 1 \rangle$.

Step 2

Calculate the New Normal ($\vec{n}_2$): The normal must be perpendicular to both $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{n}_1$. Use the Cross Product: \[ \vec{n}_2 = \vec{v} \times \vec{n}_1 = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = \langle -7, 1, 5 \rangle \]

Pick a Point: Set $t=0$ in the parametric equations to get point $P_0(1, -2, 0)$.

Step 4

Write the Final Equation: Using the point-normal form: $-7(x - 1) + 1(y + 2) + 5(z - 0) = 0$. Simplifying gives the final equation: $\mathbf{7x - y - 5z - 9 = 0}$.

Problem 04

Constant Volume of a Tetrahedron with a Moving Apex

Given the base vertices $A(1, 0, 0)$, $B(0, 1, 0)$, and $C(0, 0, 1)$. The apex $S$ is a point on the line $(D)$ with parametric equations: $$(D): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= t + 2 \\ y &= t + 2 \\ z &= t + 2 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R}$$ Calculate the volume of the tetrahedron $SABC$ and demonstrate that this volume remains constant regardless of the value of the parameter $t$.

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder]

Step 1

Define Vectors: We define vectors for the base edges from point A, and a vector from A to the apex S: \[ \overrightarrow{AB} = \langle -1, 1, 0 \rangle, \quad \overrightarrow{AC} = \langle -1, 0, 1 \rangle, \quad \overrightarrow{AS} = \langle 1+t, 2+t, 2+t \rangle \]

Step 2

Calculate the Volume using the Scalar Triple Product: The volume $V$ is given by one-sixth of the absolute value of the scalar triple product: \[ V = \frac{1}{6} |(\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AS}| \] First, the cross product: \[ \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ -1 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle \] Now, the dot product: \[ (\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}) \cdot \overrightarrow{AS} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle \cdot \langle 1+t, 2+t, 2+t \rangle = 5 + 3t \]

Step 3

Show the Volume is Constant: The normal vector to the plane $(ABC)$ is $\vec{n} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle$. The direction vector of line $(D)$ is $\vec{d} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle$. Since $\vec{n}$ is parallel to $\vec{d}$, the line $(D)$ is parallel to the plane $(ABC)$, ensuring a constant perpendicular height. Fixing $t=0$, the constant volume is: \[ V = \frac{1}{6} |5| = \frac{5}{6} \] The **volume** of the tetrahedron is $\mathbf{\frac{5}{6}}$ **cubic units**.
