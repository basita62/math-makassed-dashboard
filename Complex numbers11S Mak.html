Section 2: Writing $\cfrac{1}{z}$ (reciprocal) in algebraic form

5 Solved Examples

$a = \cfrac{8+i}{8^2 + 1^2} = \cfrac{8+i}{64+1}$

3. $a = \cfrac{1}{-3+2i}$

4. $a = \cfrac{1}{2+7i}$
$a = \cfrac{1(2-7i)}{(2+7i)(2-7i)}$
$a = \cfrac{2-7i}{2^2 + 7^2} = \cfrac{2-7i}{4+49}$
Solution: $a = \cfrac{2}{53} - \cfrac{7}{53}i$
5. $a = \cfrac{1}{-5+9i}$
$a = \cfrac{1(-5-9i)}{(-5+9i)(-5-9i)}$
$a = \cfrac{-5-9i}{(-5)^2 + 9^2} = \cfrac{-5-9i}{25+81}$

Section 6: Finding square roots of a complex number

10 Solved Examples

1. Find square roots of $Z = 3 + 4i$
Solution: $z = \pm(2+i)$
Let $z = x+iy$. $z^2 = Z \Rightarrow x^2-y^2=3$, $2xy=4$, $x^2+y^2=\sqrt{3^2+4^2}=5$
$(x^2+y^2)+(x^2-y^2)=8 \Rightarrow 2x^2=8 \Rightarrow x=\pm2$
$(x^2+y^2)-(x^2-y^2)=2 \Rightarrow 2y^2=2 \Rightarrow y=\pm1$
Since $2xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ have same signs.
2. Find square roots of $Z = 8 - 6i$
Solution: $z = \pm(3-i)$
$x^2-y^2=8$, $2xy=-6$, $x^2+y^2=\sqrt{8^2+(-6)^2}=10$
$2x^2=18 \Rightarrow x=\pm3$; $2y^2=2 \Rightarrow y=\pm1$
Since $2xy < 0$, $x$ and $y$ have opposite signs.
3. Find square roots of $Z = -5 + 12i$
Solution: $z = \pm(2+3i)$
$x^2-y^2=-5$, $2xy=12$, $x^2+y^2=13$
$2x^2=8 \Rightarrow x=\pm2$; $2y^2=18 \Rightarrow y=\pm3$
$2xy > 0 \Rightarrow$ same signs.
4. Find square roots of $Z = -7 - 24i$
Solution: $z = \pm(3-4i)$
$x^2-y^2=-7$, $2xy=-24$, $x^2+y^2=25$
$2x^2=18 \Rightarrow x=\pm3$; $2y^2=32 \Rightarrow y=\pm4$
$2xy < 0 \Rightarrow$ opposite signs.
5. Find square roots of $Z = 15 + 8i$
Solution: $z = \pm(4+i)$
$x^2-y^2=15$, $2xy=8$, $x^2+y^2=17$
$2x^2=32 \Rightarrow x=\pm4$; $2y^2=2 \Rightarrow y=\pm1$
6. Find square roots of $Z = -15 + 8i$
Solution: $z = \pm(1+4i)$
$x^2-y^2=-15$, $2xy=8$, $x^2+y^2=17$
$2x^2=2 \Rightarrow x=\pm1$; $2y^2=32 \Rightarrow y=\pm4$
7. Find square roots of $Z = 2i$
Solution: $z = \pm(1+i)$
$x^2-y^2=0$, $2xy=2$, $x^2+y^2=2$
$2x^2=2 \Rightarrow x=\pm1$; $2y^2=2 \Rightarrow y=\pm1$
8. Find square roots of $Z = -2i$
Solution: $z = \pm(1-i)$
$x^2-y^2=0$, $2xy=-2$, $x^2+y^2=2$
Since $2xy < 0$, $x$ and $y$ have opposite signs.
9. Find square roots of $Z = 11 - 60i$
Solution: $z = \pm(6-5i)$
$x^2-y^2=11$, $2xy=-60$, $x^2+y^2=61$
$2x^2=72 \Rightarrow x=\pm6$; $2y^2=50 \Rightarrow y=\pm5$
10. Find square roots of $Z = -9 + 40i$
Solution: $z = \pm(4+5i)$
$x^2-y^2=-9$, $2xy=40$, $x^2+y^2=41$
$2x^2=32 \Rightarrow x=\pm4$; $2y^2=50 \Rightarrow y=\pm5$

Section 7: Solving second degree equations $az^2 + bz + c = 0$

10 Solved Examples

1. $z^2 + z + 1 = 0$
$\Delta = 1^2 - 4(1)(1) = -3 = (i\sqrt{3})^2$
$z = \cfrac{-1 \pm i\sqrt{3}}{2}$
$S = \{-\cfrac{1}{2} \pm \cfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}i\}$

$\Delta = (-4)^2 - 4(1)(5) = -4 = (2i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{4 \pm 2i}{2}$
$S = \{2 \pm i\}$
3. $z^2 + 2z + 10 = 0$
$\Delta = 2^2 - 4(1)(10) = -36 = (6i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{-2 \pm 6i}{2}$
$S = \{-1 \pm 3i\}$
4. $2z^2 - 2z + 1 = 0$
$\Delta = (-2)^2 - 4(2)(1) = -4 = (2i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{2 \pm 2i}{4}$
$S = \{\cfrac{1}{2} \pm \cfrac{1}{2}i\}$
5. $z^2 + 9 = 0$
$z^2 = -9 \Rightarrow z = \pm \sqrt{-9}$
$S = \{-3i, 3i\}$
6. $z^2 - 2z + 2 = 0$
$\Delta = (-2)^2 - 4(1)(2) = -4 = (2i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{2 \pm 2i}{2}$
$S = \{1 \pm i\}$
7. $z^2 + 4z + 13 = 0$
$\Delta = 4^2 - 4(1)(13) = -36 = (6i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{-4 \pm 6i}{2}$
$S = \{-2 \pm 3i\}$
8. $z^2 + z + 2 = 0$
$\Delta = 1^2 - 4(1)(2) = -7 = (i\sqrt{7})^2$
$z = \cfrac{-1 \pm i\sqrt{7}}{2}$
$S = \{-\cfrac{1}{2} \pm \cfrac{\sqrt{7}}{2}i\}$

$\Delta = (-4)^2 - 4(4)(5) = -64 = (8i)^2$
$z = \cfrac{4 \pm 8i}{8}$
$S = \{\cfrac{1}{2} \pm i\}$
10. $z^2 + 1 = 0$
$z^2 = -1 \Rightarrow z = \pm \sqrt{-1}$
$S = \{-i, i\}$
