Example 1

Given: $AB = AC$, $[BE]$ bisects $\widehat{ABC}$, $[CD]$ bisects $\widehat{ACB}$.

StatementReason
1. $AB = AC$Given
2. $[BE]$ bisects $\widehat{ABC}$, $[CD]$ bisects $\widehat{ACB}$Given
3. $\widehat{ABE} = \frac{1}{2}\widehat{ABC}$ , $\widehat{ACD} = \frac{1}{2}\widehat{ACB}$Property of angle bisector
4. $\widehat{ABC} = \widehat{ACB}$In a triangle: Angles facing equal sides are equal
5. $\widehat{ABE} = \widehat{ACD}$Halves of equals are equal
6. $\widehat{A} = \widehat{A}$Common angle
7. $\triangle ABE \cong \triangle ACD$ASA Postulate
8. $BE = CD$CPOCTE
Example 2

Given: $AB = AC$, $BM = CN$, $D$ midpoint of $[AB]$, and $E$ midpoint of $[AC]$.

StatementReason
1. $AB = AC$, $BM = CN$Given
2. $D$ midpoint of $[AB]$, and $E$ midpoint of $[AC]$Given
3. $BD = \frac{1}{2}AB$ and $CE = \frac{1}{2}AC$Property of midpoint
4. $BD = CE$Halves of equals are equal
5. $BN = BM + MN$Additive Property of Length
6. $CM = CN + NM$Additive Property of Length
7. $BN = CM$Substitution
8. $\widehat{ABC} = \widehat{ACB}$In a triangle: Angles facing equal sides are equal
9. $\triangle BDN = \triangle CEM$SAS Postulate
10. $DN = EM$CPOCTE
