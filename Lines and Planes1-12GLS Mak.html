Problem 01

Equation of Plane Passing Through Three Points

Find the equation of the plane that passes through the following three points: $$A(1, 2, -1), \quad B(2, 3, 1), \quad C(3, -1, 2)$$

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder - Configure JS with ID 'ggbApplet']

Step 1

Find Two Vectors: Create vectors $\overrightarrow{AB}$ and $\overrightarrow{AC}$ using the given points: $$\overrightarrow{AB} = (2-1, 3-2, 1-(-1)) = (1, 1, 2)$$ $$\overrightarrow{AC} = (3-1, -1-2, 2-(-1)) = (2, -3, 3)$$

Step 2

Calculate the Normal Vector ($\vec{n}$): Use the Cross Product $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}$: \[ \vec{n} = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & -3 & 3 \end{vmatrix} = \langle 9, 1, -5 \rangle \]

Step 3

Write the Point-Normal Form: Use point $A(1, 2, -1)$ and the normal $\vec{n} = \langle 9, 1, -5 \rangle$: $$9(x - 1) + 1(y - 2) - 5(z + 1) = 0$$

Step 4

Final Equation: Simplify the expression: $9x - 9 + y - 2 - 5z - 5 = 0$. The final equation is: $\mathbf{9x + y - 5z - 16 = 0}$.

Problem 02

Intersection of a Line and a Plane

$\text{Find the point of intersection between the line: } (L): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= t + 3 \\ y &= -2t + 2 \\ z &= 4t - 1 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R} \\ \text{and the plane } (P): x + y + z = 10 .$

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder - Configure JS with ID 'ggbApplet2']

Step 1

Substitute Line into Plane: Plug the parametric equations of $(L)$ into the equation for $(P)$ to solve for $t$:

$$ (3 + t) + (2 - 2t) + (-1 + 4t) = 10 $$
Step 2

Solve for t: Simplify and solve the linear equation:

$$ 4 + 3t = 10 \implies 3t = 6 \implies t = 2 $$
Step 3

Find the Intersection Point: Substitute $t=2$ back into the line's equations:

$$ x = 3 + 2 = 5 $$ $$ y = 2 - 2(2) = -2 $$ $$ z = -1 + 4(2) = 7 $$

The point of intersection is $\mathbf{C(5, -2, 7)}$.

Problem 03

Equation of Line Perpendicular to a Plane Through a Point

Find the parametric equations of the line $(D)$ that passes through the point $A(1, 2, 3)$ and is perpendicular to the plane $(P): 4x - y + 2z = 5$.

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder - Configure JS with ID 'ggbApplet3']

Step 1

Identify Direction Vector: The normal vector of the plane $(P)$, $\vec{n} = (4, -1, 2)$, is parallel to the required line $(D)$. Therefore, $\vec{v}_D = (4, -1, 2)$ is our direction vector.

Step 2

Use the Given Point: The line passes through $A(1, 2, 3)$.

Step 3

Write Parametric Equations:Combine the point $A$ and the direction vector $\vec{v}_D$ to form the equations:

$(D): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= 4t + 1 \\ y &= -t + 2 \\ z &= 2t + 3 \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R}$
Problem 04

Angle Between Two Planes

Find the angle $\theta$ between the two planes $(P_1)$ and $(P_2)$: \[ (P_1): x + y + z = 1 \] \[ (P_2): x - 2y + 3z = 1 \]

[GeoGebra Applet Figure Placeholder - Configure JS with ID 'ggbApplet4']

Step 1

Extract Normal Vectors: The normal vectors are $\vec{n}_1 = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle$ and $\vec{n}_2 = \langle 1, -2, 3 \rangle$.

Step 2

Use the Dot Product Formula:

$$\text{The angle } \theta \text{ between the planes is found using the formula } \cos(\theta) = \frac{|\vec{n}_1 \cdot \vec{n}_2|}{\|\vec{n}_1\| \|\vec{n}_2\|}.$$

$$\vec{n}_1 \cdot \vec{n}_2 = (1)(1) + (1)(-2) + (1)(3) = 1 - 2 + 3 = 2$$

$$\|\vec{n}_1\| = \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{3}$$

$$\|\vec{n}_2\| = \sqrt{1^2 + (-2)^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 9} = \sqrt{14}$$

Step 3

Calculate the Angle:

$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}\sqrt{14}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{42}}$$

$$\theta = \arccos\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{42}}\right) \approx \arccos(0.3086)$$

$$\text{The angle is approximately } \mathbf{72.03^\circ}.$$

Practice Problems

Solve the following problems on your own. Click each question box to reveal instructions and the final answer.

Practice 01: Equation of Plane Through Three Points

Find the general equation of the plane $(\Pi)$ that passes through the three non-collinear points: $$P(1, 0, 0), \quad Q(0, 2, 0), \quad R(0, 0, 3)$$

Instructions: Form vectors $\overrightarrow{PQ}$ and $\overrightarrow{PR}$. Calculate their cross product to find the plane's normal vector $\vec{n}$. Use the point-normal form with point $P$ to get the final equation.

Final Answer: $\mathbf{6x + 3y + 2z - 6 = 0}$

Practice 02: Intersection of a Line and a Plane

Find the coordinates of the point where the line $(L)$ intersects the plane $(P)$: $$(L): x = 2 - t, \quad y = 1 + 3t, \quad z = 4$$ $$(P): 3x + y - 2z = 5$$

Instructions: Substitute the line's parametric equations into the plane's equation. Solve the resulting linear equation for $t$. Plug this value of $t$ back into the line equations to find the intersection point $(x, y, z)$.

Final Answer: $\mathbf{(0, 7, 4)}$

Practice 03: Line Perpendicular to a Plane Through a Point

Find the parametric equations of the line $(D)$ that passes through the origin $O(0, 0, 0)$ and is perpendicular to the plane $(P): x + 2y - z = 10$.

Instructions: The normal vector of the plane $\vec{n} = (1, 2, -1)$ serves as the direction vector for the line $(D)$. Use the given point $O(0, 0, 0)$ to write the final parametric equations.

Final Answer: $(D): \left\{ \begin{aligned} x &= t \\ y &= 2t \\ z &= -t \end{aligned} \right. \text{ , } t\in\mathbb{R}$

Practice 04: Angle Between Two Planes

Find the angle $\theta$ between the two planes $(P_1): 3x - y + 2z = 1$ and $(P_2): x + 4y - z = 2$.

Instructions: Extract the normal vectors $\vec{n}_1$ and $\vec{n}_2$. Use the formula $\cos(\theta) = \frac{|\vec{n}_1 \cdot \vec{n}_2|}{\|\vec{n}_1\| \|\vec{n}_2\|}$. Calculate the dot products and magnitudes to find the angle in degrees.

Final Answer: $\mathbf{\theta = 90^\circ}$ (The planes are perpendicular).
